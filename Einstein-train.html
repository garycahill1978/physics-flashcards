Einstein Train Simultaneity Thought Experiment

Two lightning strikes hit the front and back of the train. The key question is not only when the flashes arrive, but what each observer infers about when the strikes occurred.

Observer

Train speed

Light speed stays constant.

Different observers disagree about simultaneity.

What the train observer says

The observer sits midway between the lightning strikes.

Light reaches them simultaneously.

Therefore:
the strikes were simultaneous in the train frame.

What the platform observer says

The lightning strikes are fixed platform events.

The train observer moves right after the strikes.

So flash B has less distance to travel, while flash A has more distance to travel.

Therefore B reaches the moving observer first.

Core insight

There is no universal NOW.

Different observers divide spacetime into different present moments.
